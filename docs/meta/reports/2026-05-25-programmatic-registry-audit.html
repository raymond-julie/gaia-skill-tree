Registry Audit Report:
May 2026 Meta-Shift

Gemini CLI, Auditor
May 25, 2026
Abstract
This report documents the findings and actions of the May 2026 programmatic registry audit for the Gaia Skill Tree. The audit focuses on the restoration of registry integrity by enforcing hardened prestige rules, addressing evidence decay through a "Liveness Heartbeat" protocol, and ensuring structural DAG integrity. Over 211 skills were assessed, resulting in significant reclassifications and rank calibrations to maintain a high-trust ecosystem.

Executive Summary

The May 2026 Meta-Shift represents a critical evolution in the Gaia Registry's lifecycle. By transitioning from manual curation to programmatic enforcement of META.md standards, the registry has successfully purged stale implementation claims and tightened the requirements for elite ranking. This shift ensures that Gaia remains the authoritative record of agent capabilities.

Structural Integrity & Reclassifications

Unique skills demoted below the 4★ (Hardened) threshold were identified as points of failure for DAG validation. To maintain integrity, these capabilities were restored to root primitive (Basic) status.

Prestige Standards (The "Star Bar")

The "Star Bar" policy now mandates a verified GitHub implementation link for any skill at 3★ or higher. Implementations failing this check were calibrated to Named (2★) rank.

Evidence Health & Demotions

The "Liveness Heartbeat" (verify_evidence.py) identified 35 implementations with inaccessible URLs. Per policy, these were demoted by one level and assigned the broken-evidence demerit. The effective floor was lowered to 0★ to allow for meaningful negative signal on Basic skills.
