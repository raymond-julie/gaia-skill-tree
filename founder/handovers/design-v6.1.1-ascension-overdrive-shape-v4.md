## Final shape

> **One antique medallion chassis, two paired stellar cosmologies.**

The fork no longer separates botanical art from unrelated cosmic posters. It separates two outcomes of the same stellar tree:

- **Suite emits outward:** growth becomes stellar mass, a mature burning star, then a supernova Yggdrasil.
- **Unique collapses inward:** the same tree lineage bends around a void, an accretion field, then an impossible singularity.

Rank color is the primary identity. Branch material and cosmological behavior identify class. Brass is the shared family material.

The terminal contrast is deliberately simple:

- Suite 6★ = expansion.
- Unique 6★ = collapse.

This opposition must read with labels hidden. The family resemblance must remain when the rails are viewed separately.

## Shared medallion grammar

C and D use one locked construction template from 4★ through 6★:

- Same circular or very slightly oval silhouette.
- Same diameter, crop, camera, apparent depth, lighting direction, visual center, and four cardinal registration points.
- Same three-part enclosure: antique outer edge, rank-color enamel or patina band, inner image field.
- Same engraved line weight, relief depth, shadow behavior, and restrained brass specular.
- Same tree occupancy: approximately 60%–68% of the inner field.
- Same ring occupancy: approximately 8%–11% of the diameter.
- Same hero, card, and badge optical sizing within each paired rank.
- No readable text or baked-in star count as the only rank signal; stars remain semantic HTML.

Suite may preserve complete rings and radiate beyond them. Unique may interrupt a ring or create one impossible crossing, but cannot adopt a new chassis or poster silhouette.

Rank color occupies roughly 20%–30% of the visible medallion face through enamel, core, corona, fracture, or illuminated relief. It cannot be reduced to a faint rim.

## Suite ladder: stellar ascension

Ranks 1★–3★ retain the existing botanical growth narrative, refined to lead naturally into stellar scale. Ranks 4★–6★ are no longer botanical specimens; they are increasingly massive stellar-tree events.

### 1★ Awakened — stellar seed

A single shoot rises from a compact root knot. Cyan ignition appears where root becomes trunk. The silhouette is vertical and badge-legible, with no prop, specimen card, tool, candle, signet, altar, or secondary emblem.

### 2★ Named — structured sapling

The shoot gains paired boughs and a stable crown. Teal energy settles inside the trunk, optionally contained by one faint orbital or naming ring. It feels organized, not merely taller.

### 3★ Evolved — Y-crowned young tree

The trunk thickens into a legible Y-shaped crown. Violet light travels from roots to both arms. Coordinates may sit behind the tree, but it cannot read as a pressed botanical illustration.

### 4★ Extra — young-tree dwarf star

A compact young tree behaves like a dwarf star. Its fuchsia-white core compresses the crown into a strong circular mass; dense corona, energized roots, and visible stellar pressure shift the image from botany to astronomy.

`--rank-4` fuchsia must own a continuous enamel, core, or corona region. The image remains contained except for a restrained edge-touching corona.

### 5★ Ultimate — burning-gold mature tree-star

A mature, broad-crowned tree burns as a stable gold star. It is not a tree on fire: branches become solar prominences, roots become radiating stellar structure, and the trunk remains the readable axis.

`--rank-5` and the established gold role dominate. One or two prominences may breach the rim. Increased mass, heat, and radiance replace added columns, gates, or ornamental architecture.

### 6★ Apex — supernova Yggdrasil

A complete Yggdrasil becomes a white-hot supernova while its roots, trunk, and crown remain readable through the event. This is the largest scale in the ladder, not a more decorated medal.

Use the canonical `DESIGN.md` Level VI recipe as light behavior:

- Brilliant white specular core, boosted above standard ranks.
- Spectral hue cycle through cyan, violet, amber/gold, and fuchsia.
- Orbit sparks or ejecta distributed as restrained spectral accents.
- Gold convergence at the outer fringe so the whole still reads as Apex gold at a glance.
- Shining-bright-white tree, not a generic rainbow fill or gradient text.

Controlled ejecta, shock rings, sparks, and corona may breach the medallion. The brass chassis must remain inferable, and the effects must stay inside the rail safe area.

### Suite sequence test

With copy and stars hidden, C1–C6 must read:

`ignition → identity → structural growth → dwarf star → mature burning star → supernova world tree`

If C4–C6 read only as young tree, old tree, ornate tree, the commission has failed.

## Unique ladder: gravitational impossibility

D retains the ambition and legacy color grading of the old Unique concept art while joining C's chassis. The legacy images are mood, scale, and cosmology references—not silhouettes to paste into V4.

### 4★ Unique — rooted void tree

The paired 4★ chassis holds a young tree rooted around a small void. Roots pass behind and in front of the void in one bold, legible impossible relationship.

Fuchsia `--rank-4` remains prominent in enamel, corona, or fracture edges. `--tier-unique` violet and cold void material identify the branch without replacing rank color.

### 5★ Unique Ultimate — gravitational tree

A mature tree bends around a gold accretion ring. Limbs and roots share an orbit while the trunk preserves the common silhouette; one branch may re-enter from the wrong side.

Gold `--rank-5` identifies level. Violet lensing and cold fractures identify Unique. It collapses inward while Suite 5★ expands as a burning star.

### 6★ Unique Impossible — singularity Yggdrasil

A complete Yggdrasil surrounds a white singularity. Crown becomes root through one bold impossible loop that reads even at card size. The singularity is the brightest point; the tree remains dark and cold, rim-lit by gold, white, and restrained violet/spectral fractures.

The ring may be interrupted but remains inferable. Asset I must evolve directly from this seal rather than introducing an unrelated terminal object.

### Pairing test

In grayscale, C4/D4, C5/D5, and C6/D6 must still match in diameter, enclosure, anchors, line weight, subject occupancy, and tree ancestry. If D reads as a poster beside a coin, it fails.

## Color system

Two color axes remain distinct:

- **Rank color = earned level and primary medallion identity.**
- **Branch material/cosmology = Suite or Unique class.**

| Rank | Required role |
|---|---|
| 1★ | `--rank-1` cyan enamel and ignition |
| 2★ | `--rank-2` teal enamel and stable core |
| 3★ | `--rank-3` violet energy and Y-crown |
| 4★ | `--rank-4` fuchsia enamel, core, corona, or fracture |
| 5★ | `--rank-5` gold stellar or accretion material |
| 6★ | `--rank-6`, white specular core, spectral cycle, gold fringe |

5★ and 6★ share a gold base but differ in behavior: 5★ is stable gold combustion; 6★ adds the canonical white point, hue cycle, orbit sparks, and expanded gold fringe.

Do not wash Suite into one brass set or Unique into one violet set. Production helpers must sample generated CSS tokens; do not create a parallel hand-entered palette.

## Supporting asset direction

### B V4 — quiet copperplate substrate

Keep the dark astrolabe backdrop and its copperplate provenance, but reduce its insistence:

- Compress contrast and remove equal-weight line noise, scratches, and bright nodes.
- Retain one or two large astrolabe arcs as low-contrast structure.
- Create quiet fields behind title, medallions, fork copy, paired rails, and terminal copy.
- Make copper geometry feel printed into the ground, never illuminated above it.
- Keep it recognizable in full view but subordinate in every scene.

Start with masks, frequency cleanup, and regrading of the current lossless master. Regenerate only if disciplined reprocessing cannot create quiet fields.

### F1–F3 V4 — subject-free atmospheric fields

Keep the liked blue, teal, and violet atmosphere. Remove every central prop or competing subject: candle, signet, compass, specimen, altar, tool, emblem, or tree.

Fill removed areas with plausible continuation of dust, rays, coordinates, and peripheral texture. The C medallion becomes the sole subject. F4–F6 are retained only if their architecture stays subordinate and does not introduce a second explosion behind C6.

### H V4 — true symmetric Y-fork

H is a measured vector/layout problem, not an image-generation problem:

- Center the trunk on the composition box.
- Use one fork point, mirrored tangents, and equal apparent branch lengths.
- Align endpoints to the optical centers of equal-width Suite and Unique rails.
- Use equal outer insets and one fixed central gutter.
- Share control points between SVG/runtime thread and raster fallback.
- Allow ornamental asymmetry only after the structural skeleton passes a mirror overlay.
- Keep all Unique art and external effects inside its rail safe margin.

Mobile preserves the explicit fork and the ratified `→ Unique` cue while stacking rails Suite first, Unique second.

### Apex Gate — keep architecture, rebuild predicate micro-seals

The arch, six predicates, latch sequence, and terminal scene stay. The small white-background line-art images do not.

Replace them with six matched transparent brass micro-seals:

- True alpha, clean edges, no white ground or halo.
- One strong silhouette per predicate, with at most two or three interior cuts at served size.
- Common viewBox, optical size, brass/dark-ink treatment, and minimum stroke/gap width.
- Legible at the actual CSS size, not only in the master.
- Supporting marks for code and label, not a new icon family.

Prefer extraction, simplified vector tracing, and alpha cleanup over full regeneration.

## Geometry, effects, mobile, and motion

- Build Act III from one centered inner composition box with equal rails, one gutter, and equal outer safe margins.
- Render paired C/D medallions at the same box size and vertical registration.
- Put off-medallion effects in bounded, absolute effect layers that do not affect grid measurement or create overflow.
- 4★ remains contained; 5★ may reach roughly 4%–6% beyond the medal; 6★ may reach roughly 10%–14%.
- At 1440, 900, 768, 390, and 375 widths, neither branch, seal, clip path, nor effect crosses the section safe edge.
- Below 900px, release sticky pins and use the existing static stack. Author card/badge/mobile variants; do not rely on browser downscaling of hero masters.
- Reduce effect reach and density on mobile instead of clipping the desktop effect.
- Suite motion expands or radiates slowly; Unique motion pulls or lenses inward slowly.
- Keep the tree silhouette stable while effects move around it.
- Pause ambient loops offscreen; bound paint areas; do not depend on `mix-blend-mode`.
- Reduced motion uses a composed static peak frame with fully drawn paths and unchanged meaning.

## Production method

Use programmatic helpers for token-driven grading, shared crop and occupancy, alpha/matte cleanup, edge decontamination, viewBox normalization, contact sheets, WebP export, and dimension/palette/safe-edge checks.

Use structural regeneration or image editing for C4–C6 and D4–D6. C1–C3 may be edited or regenerated only as needed to enter the same stellar lineage. Inpaint F1–F3 to subtract their props while preserving the fields.

Do not regenerate H, the Apex Gate architecture/logic, B before a reprocessing proof, or the full F1–F3 backdrops.

Lock the chassis first. Generate/review in pairs—C4/D4, C5/D5, C6/D6—before filling the remaining ladder. Preserve lossless masters, prompts, masks, seeds, LUTs, helpers, and contact sheets outside the repository. Only optimized WebP raster enters the repo; native SVG stays SVG.

## Acceptance and failure tests

1. **Family:** all C4–C6 and D4–D6 assets visibly share one chassis at equal size.
2. **Scale:** Suite reads dwarf star → mature burning star → supernova Yggdrasil, not botanical ornament escalation.
3. **Opposition:** Suite 6★ expands and Unique 6★ collapses with labels hidden.
4. **Rank:** each 1★–6★ medallion is identifiable by canonical rank color at 240px without star count.
5. **White point:** only 6★ uses full white-hot light; C6's brightest point is the tree and D6's is the singularity.
6. **Backdrop:** F1–F3 have no central subject; B remains recognizable but subordinate.
7. **Geometry:** the stripped post-fork skeleton passes a mirrored overlay; rail bounds, fork tangents, centers, and safe margins align.
8. **Small image:** all six Apex seals have transparent backgrounds and remain legible at served size.
9. **Terminal kinship:** Asset I reads as an evolved D6, not a new art family.
10. **Responsive:** no art or effect crosses safe edges or causes horizontal overflow at required widths.
11. **Accessibility:** meaning survives grayscale, reduced motion, and hidden decorative art.
12. **Alpha/performance:** no matte halos, persistent `will-change`, offscreen loops, or unbounded paint.
